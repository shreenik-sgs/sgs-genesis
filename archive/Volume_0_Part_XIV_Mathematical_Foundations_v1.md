# Volume 0 - Part XIV: Mathematical Foundations & Canonical Models

## Canonical Mathematical Domains
- **Graph Theory**: Represent systems and dependencies.
- **Network Science**: Study topology, centrality, resilience, contagion.
- **Causal Inference**: Differentiate causation from correlation.
- **Bayesian Reasoning**: Update beliefs as evidence accumulates.
- **Information Theory**: Measure information gain and uncertainty.
- **Optimization**: Allocate scarce capital under constraints.
- **Time Series Analysis**: Characterize temporal evolution.
- **Decision Theory**: Select actions under uncertainty.
- **Probability Theory**: Model stochastic outcomes.
- **Simulation**: Evaluate alternative futures.

## Normative Requirements
- Every recommendation MUST identify its causal chain.
- Every confidence update SHOULD be evidence-driven.
- Optimization objectives MUST be explicit.
- Simulation outputs MUST preserve assumptions.
- Historical replay MUST use identical mathematical definitions.
