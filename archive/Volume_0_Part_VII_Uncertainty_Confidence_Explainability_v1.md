# Volume 0 - Part VII: Uncertainty, Confidence & Explainability

## Core Principles
- Uncertainty is a first-class property of every canonical object.
- Confidence is earned through evidence, not asserted.
- Contradictory evidence is preserved rather than discarded.
- Confidence and uncertainty evolve over time.
- Every recommendation must be explainable end-to-end.

## Constitutional Rules
- No opaque scoring systems.
- Confidence without evidence is prohibited.
- Low-confidence recommendations remain visible but clearly identified.
- Contradictory evidence cannot be hidden.
- Historical calibration is mandatory.
